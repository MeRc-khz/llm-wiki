Quantum Mechanix is a high-concept, cyberpunk noir story premise centered on a protagonist named Kirk Kilohertz, a man who is tragically trapped phasing through the multiverse.

The Protagonist and His Abilities
Kirk is an incredibly intelligent individual who suffers from bouts of amnesia and has high-functioning autism (Asperger's Syndrome). He has learned to compensate for this by becoming an excellent communicator with extreme, almost hyper-level charisma. Through his immense focus and years of quiet devotion to spiritual teachings and tantric meditation, Kirk unknowingly unlocked the secrets of traveling to parallel universes, having "pleased the GOD". In his current universe, he works out of the "Quantum Garage" as a former melee fighter turned private investigator who fixes electric vehicles, such as quadcopters and motorcycles.

The Tragic Flaw and the Multiverse Trigger
The core irony of Kirk's existence is his "flaw": his overwhelmingly positive outlook on life and his charismatic vibration strongly attract women to him. This is a severe problem because sexual ecstasy and orgasm serve as an involuntary trigger that causes him to time travel or "slip through" to another universe. This "smash" trigger resets his timeline, tearing him further and farther away from his original universe, his family, his friends, and his true love, whose memory haunts him.

The Constants and the Mission
Despite his constant displacement, there are three constants anchoring his existence in every universe:
1. His boss (a car dealership owner)
2. His bike, known as the "Customer Chopper"
3. Room 112 at the US Grant Hotel

Narrative notes indicate that Kirk uses a private studio in his garage to play old, unreleased "time-traveling dilating tracks" on vinyl records to pinpoint specific moments in the past—such as an onstage incident twenty years prior where his crew mysterious disappeared. However, his attempts to gather clues and solve the case are constantly complicated by accidental time-travel "slip-throughs" when he encounters women, stranded in a cycle of multidimensional displacement.

Format and Aesthetic
The creator's notes frame the world as a "Future now" setting heavily influenced by NLP, spirituality, and tech, even featuring fictional drugs called "sex packets". The project is brainstormed to be delivered in short, episodic micro-formats (5-10 minute episodes) with accompanying "comic strip style" social media buzz and panel trails.
